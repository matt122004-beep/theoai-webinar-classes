The most influential letter in Christian history was not written by Paul. It was written by his secretary.

We call them Paul's letters. Not Tertius's. Not Timothy's. Paul's.

The anxiety of our age

If a machine can generate prose, what happens to authorship?

I want to suggest we've been asking the wrong questions.

Selected Messages, Book 1, p. 21
It is not the words of the Bible that are inspired, but the men that were inspired. Inspiration acts not on the man's words or his expressions but on the man himself.
— Ellen G. White, 1886

The principle

God's penmen,
not His pen

What IS inspired

The person. The ideas. The theological vision. The convictions.

What is NOT

The specific words. The sentence structure. The grammar. The arrangement.

An Operational Reality
~20
literary assistants employed by Ellen White

Secretaries, copyists, and editors who corrected her grammar, restructured her sentences, reorganized her chapters, and transposed paragraphs between manuscripts.

"I am not a scholar" and "not a grammarian."
— E. G. WHITE, SELECTED MESSAGES 3:90

The Bookmaker

Marian Davis (1879–1904)

  • 📚
    Gathered & assembled — pulled from White's diaries, letters, sermons, and articles to compile book manuscripts
  • 📐
    Structured chapters — planned layouts, decided how much space to devote to each topic
  • ✂️
    Edited for clarity — dropped needless words, simplified sentences, rewrote openings
  • 📖
    The Desire of Ages — the most beloved Adventist book, assembled by Davis from White's writings

"She is my bookmaker."
— ELLEN WHITE

The Rebel

Fannie Bolton

A talented writer who couldn't make peace with the arrangement. Hired and fired three times.

Bolton's Demand

Acknowledge your editors publicly
Give credit to sources you draw from
Follow secular literary conventions
VS

White's Model

Authority rests on the thought-originator
The vision, not the vocabulary, is what matters
Follows the biblical, prophetic model
White Estate Document File 445, 1901
I thank God that He has kept Sister White from following my supposed superior wisdom and righteousness, and has kept her from acknowledging editors or authors; but has given to the people the unadulterated expression of God's mind.
— Fannie Bolton

Bolton came around. The gap between who produced the words and who owned the ideas is exactly the gap AI has now blown wide open.

The Crisis of Our Moment

Universities. Newsrooms. Churches.

Everyone is asking: "Who actually wrote this?"

The assumption beneath the question: that the physical act of producing words is what matters most.

Ellen White would have found this assumption baffling.

📝

Davis rewrote

openings, restructured chapters, dropped words

🔄

Assistants transposed

sentences between manuscripts, fixed grammar

✅

White reviewed

"I read over all that is copied, to see that everything is as it should be."

The question we should be asking

Not "Did you type every word?"

But "Are the ideas yours?"

Do you understand the argument?

Can you defend it?

Did you direct the thinking?

The Crucial Distinction

Two ways to use AI

AI as Marian Davis

A tool that organizes, articulates, and polishes ideas the human already holds
You direct the thinking
You review every word
= Authorship by every biblical standard
VS

AI as Substitute

A replacement for having ideas at all
The machine does the thinking
You accept whatever it produces
= Not authorship. Not yours.

The Insight

AI automates the pen-holding

What AI can do

Generate sentences. Structure paragraphs. Clean grammar. Suggest phrasing. Gather and arrange.

What AI cannot do

Originate theological conviction. Wrestle with God. Have apostolic authority. Form a moral argument that is yours.

An extraordinarily efficient Marian Davis.
It gathers. It arranges. It polishes.

AI is not the disease.
AI is the diagnostic.

It has revealed that we were already measuring the wrong thing. Students have been stringing together sentences they didn't understand for generations — long before ChatGPT made it easier.

The Editor's Craft
I have tried to begin the chapters and paragraphs with short sentences, and indeed to simplify wherever possible, to drop out every needless word, and to make the work...more compact and vigorous.
— Marian Davis to W. C. White, August 9, 1897

She was doing what any good editor does — and what any good AI writing tool does. The difference is only a matter of mechanism.

The questions that have always mattered

Whose ideas are they?

Are they true? Are they faithful?
Do they reflect a mind that has
actually done the wrestling?

If the answer is yes, then the pen that wrote them — whether it belonged to Tertius, Marian Davis, or a large language model — is beside the point.

One might note, with some amusement, that Tertius does break protocol at the end of Romans by interjecting his own greeting. Even the secretary wanted credit — and Paul apparently let him have it.
